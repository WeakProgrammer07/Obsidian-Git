#### Intro Paragraph Draft
In recent years we have seen facial recognition technology transition from a futuristic concept to become something that is seemingly everywhere, deployed by law enforcement and private entities. Advocates of facial recognition often point to how it is able to help identify suspects and solve criminal cases. However, underneath all of this success is a deeply flawed system which infringes upon the privacy of individuals and even when it appears to be working is often biased against minorities. Due to the unregulated nature, the clear algorithmic biases, and its harmful capabilities if misused, facial recognition should be banned from usage in public spaces to protect our privacy and freedoms.
