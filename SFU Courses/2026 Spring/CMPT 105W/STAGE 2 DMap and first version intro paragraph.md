### Intro Paragraph Draft
In recent years we have seen facial recognition technology transition from a futuristic concept to become something that is seemingly everywhere, deployed by law enforcement and private entities. Advocates of facial recognition often point to how it is able to help identify suspects and solve criminal cases. However, underneath all of this success is a deeply flawed system which infringes upon the privacy of individuals and even when it appears to be working is often biased against minorities. Due to the unregulated nature, the clear algorithmic biases, and its harmful capabilities if misused, facial recognition should be banned from usage in public spaces to protect our privacy and freedoms. 
### Pro Reasons
##### The Privacy Violation
- The constant scanning of crowds captures the biometric data of innocent civilians without their explicit consent, knowledge, or a reasonable expectation of being tracked (Hirose, 2017).
- Even though individuals are in a public space, they do not consent to being permanently tracked and cataloged by a database. Because this violates fundamental privacy rights, the technology should be banned.
##### Algorithmic Bias and Discrimination
- Studies on commercial gender classification algorithms show significant accuracy disparities, with darker-skinned females being misidentified at much higher rates than lighter-skinned males. (Buolamwini & Gebru, 2018)
- Deploying biased algorithms in public spaces inevitably leads to the unjust targeting, harassment, or false arrest of marginalized groups. A technology this discriminatory is too dangerous and harmful for public use.
##### The Civil Liberties Threat
- Civil liberties organizations argue that attempting to regulate the technology is futile because any level of mass biometric tracking creates a chilling effect on free speech and peaceful assembly (Marissa & Cagle, 2024)
- Because the technology acts as an ultimate tool for government overreach and control, mere regulation is insufficient. A ban is the only way to protect civil liberties.

### Con Reasons
##### Efficiency in Law Enforcement
- Police forces using the technology have reported significant operational successes, such as the Metropolitan Police attributing 1,000 arrests directly to the use of facial recognition cameras (Jessup, 2025)
- Banning the technology would remove the police of a working efficient tool, which advocates argue makes public spaces more dangerous by allowing criminals to be present.
##### Regulation as a Better Alternative to a Ban
- Academic research into the intersection of big data and ethics suggests that robust data protection laws, algorithmic auditing, and strict usage guidelines can safely manage biometric surveillance (Wang et al., 2024)
- It is reactionary to ban a helpful tool outright. By implementing strict legal boundaries, society can benefit from the security aspects of the technology while mitigating the privacy risks
##### Modernizing Security Infrastructure
- Modern data analysis and big data frameworks allow for the rapid processing of security information and threat detection at a scale that human operators simply cannot match (Wang et al., 2024)
- As cities grow and threats evolve, security systems must adapt. Banning advanced artificial intelligence tools leaves public safety stuck in the past, making society vulnerable to modern threats that require rapid, automated responses
