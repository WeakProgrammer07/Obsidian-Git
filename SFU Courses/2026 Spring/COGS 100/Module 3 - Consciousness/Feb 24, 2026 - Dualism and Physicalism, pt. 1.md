- From the mid 1600's to the early 1900's the study of mind = the study of consciousness
	- mind = consciousness
	- In western tradition of studying mind
	- Consciousness = awareness, thought, experience, self awareness
- In the early 1900's development into behaviourism was more popular than inquiry into consciousness
- Awareness and experience, behaviourists argued, couldn't be studied scientifically
- As cognitive science developed this re-opened inquiry into the mind
	- Perception tells us about sensory experience
	- Language tells us about the structure of thought
	- Memory tells us about what we were and were not aware of
- Cognitive Science (what we have studied so far), does not tell us about consciousness
- Cognitive Science studies some internal aspects (structure and processes of thought) but not all (subjective character or quality of thought)
- Possible features of consciousness
	- Sentience - Sensing and responding to the world
	- Awakeness - Being awake, alert, and responsive to the world
	- Self-Consciousness
	- "What is like"-ness
	- First person experience
- Dualism
	- The mind and body are two fundamentally different things and different kinds of substances
	- The body is a physical substance. The mind is a non-physical substance
- Physicalism
	- The mind, just like the body, is a physical substance. 
	- These are not distinct things – the mind is part of the physical body.
