#### **Written Question Guides**

**1. Sole Prop vs. Corp:**
* **Liability:** A sole proprietorship has unlimited liability, meaning the owner is personally responsible for all business debts. A corporation has limited liability, meaning the owners' liability is limited to their investment.
* **Taxation:** Sole proprietors are taxed as individuals, and business losses can be deducted from personal income. Corporations face "double taxation," where the corporation pays tax on profits, and shareholders pay personal tax on dividends received.
* **Funds:** Corporations find it easier to raise money by selling stock, whereas sole proprietorships find it difficult to raise funds.

**2. Ghost Kitchens:**
* **Definition:** Ghost kitchens are delivery-only food preparation facilities with no public-facing storefront and no in-store dining.
* **Benefits:**
    1.  **Capacity:** They allow businesses to maximize kitchen capacity use, such as a breakfast restaurant operating as a burger concept in the evening.
    2.  **Flexibility:** Owners can easily add or remove menu items on delivery apps and adjust operating hours.
    3.  **Costs:** They have lower rent, insurance, and staffing costs compared to traditional restaurants.

**3. Porter's Forces (Example):**
* **Buyers:** In the Canadian grocery industry, buyers have significant power because five companies control 80% of food sales, giving them immense leverage over food producers.
* **Substitutes:** The rise of online banking provided a substitute for traditional bill payment, leading to a significant decline in mail volume and revenue for Canada Post.

**4. CSR Stances:**
* **Definition:** Corporate Social Responsibility refers to how a business balances its commitments to individuals and groups in its external environment.
* **Obstructionist:** Doing as little as possible to solve problems and potentially denying or covering up wrongdoing, such as Volkswagen falsifying emissions tests.
* **Defensive:** Doing everything that is legally required, but nothing more.
* **Accommodative:** Meeting legal and ethical requirements and going further if asked.
* **Proactive:** Actively seeking opportunities to contribute to society and viewing themselves as good corporate citizens, such as companies that set up charitable foundations.

**5. Marketing Mix:**
* **Target Market:** A company selects one or more market segments (categories of customer types) as its target market.
* **4 Ps:** The marketing mix consists of Product (the good or service), Price (how much it sells for), Place (distribution), and Promotion (communicating information).
* **Relation:** A company must adapt its product to fit the target market—a process called product positioning—and communicate the nature of the product to that market.

### **Question 1**
**Explain the distinction between "unethical" and "illegal" behavior in a business context, and provide an example of a behavior that fits into the category of "unethical but legal."**

**Answer:**
A critical distinction exists between unethical and illegal acts. Ethical behavior conforms to individual beliefs and social norms about what is right, while unethical behavior is defined by those same beliefs and norms as wrong. Illegal behavior is determined by the law. A behavior can be unethical but still legal. An example of this is paying very low wages in a foreign country where it is permitted by law, but considered unethical by many standards. Another example is hiring a less-qualified friend or relative, which may be legal but is often considered unethical.

### **Question 2**
**Describe the four phases of the "Business Cycle" and define what constitutes a "recession."**

**Answer:**
The business cycle is the pattern of short-term ups and downs in an economy. It consists of four phases:
1.  **Peak:** The high point of economic activity.
2.  **Recession:** A period of economic shrinkage. A recession is typically defined as two consecutive quarters of decline in an economy's GDP.
3.  **Trough:** The low point of economic activity.
4.  **Recovery:** The period where the economy begins to grow again.

### **Question 3**
**What is "National Competitive Advantage"? List the four conditions that contribute to it.**

**Answer:**
National Competitive Advantage is a theory suggesting that a nation's advantage in international trade arises from a combination of four specific conditions:
1.  **Factor Conditions:** The availability of labor, capital, and natural resources.
2.  **Demand Conditions:** The presence of a strong domestic consumer base.
3.  **Related and Supporting Industries:** The existence of strong local supplier networks.
4.  **Strategies, Structures, and Rivalries:** The presence of intense domestic competition that fosters innovation.

### **Question 4**
**Define "Business Incubators" and explain their impact on the survival rate of new businesses.**

**Answer:**
Business Incubators are facilities that nurture new businesses by providing them with office space, legal and accounting advice, and business contacts. They provide a supportive environment for startups to grow. The impact on survival is significant: businesses using incubators have an 80% survival rate after five years.

### **Question 5**
**Define "Corporate Culture" and explain why it is difficult to change, using the RCMP as an example.**

**Answer:**
Corporate culture is the unique identity of a company, defined as the shared experiences, stories, beliefs, and norms that characterize it—essentially "the way we do things around here". Changing an organization's culture is extremely difficult because it involves altering deeply ingrained values and behaviors. The RCMP serves as an example of this difficulty; it has struggled for over a decade to reform what has been described as a "horribly broken" and "toxic" culture of harassment and discrimination, despite efforts to change it.

### **Question 6**
**Differentiate between "National Brands" and "Private Brands" and provide an example of each.**

**Answer:**
* **National Brands** are products that are produced and distributed by the manufacturer and carry the manufacturer's name. An example is *Chips Ahoy!* cookies.
* **Private Brands** (often called private labels) carry the retailer's own brand name rather than the manufacturer's. An example is Loblaws' *President's Choice* line.

### **Question 7**
**What is the role of a "Chartered Professional Accountant" (CPA) in Canada, and what three legacy designations were unified under this title?**

**Answer:**
A Chartered Professional Accountant (CPA) is the unified accounting designation in Canada. CPAs provide services such as auditing, tax planning, and management consulting. The designation unified the three traditional accounting designations: Chartered Accountant (CA), Certified General Accountant (CGA), and Certified Management Accountant (CMA).

### **Question 8**
**Explain the concept of "Supply Chain Management" (SCM) and identifying two key benefits of effective SCM.**

**Answer:**
Supply Chain Management (SCM) is the principle of managing the supply chain as a whole—treating the flow of information, materials, and services from raw material suppliers to end customers as a coordinated system—rather than as a collection of individual companies acting in their own interest.
Key benefits include:
1.  **Reduced Inventories:** Sharing information allows members to keep lower stock levels.
2.  **Faster Supply Times:** Coordination helps avoid delays and smooths the flow of materials.
*(Alternative benefits include lowered costs and better value for the customer).*

### **Question 9**
**Who are "Whistle-Blowers" and what risks do they typically face?**

**Answer:**
A whistle-blower is an employee who publicizes a company's unethical, illegal, or socially irresponsible actions. They often expose issues like fraud or safety violations. The risks they face are significant; whistle-blowers often face demotion or termination of their employment as retaliation for their actions.

### **Question 10**
**Define the role of a "Lobbyist" and explain how the Canadian government regulates their activity.**

**Answer:**
A lobbyist is a person hired by a company or industry to represent its interests with government officials. Their goal is to influence government policy to favor their clients. The Canadian government regulates this activity through the federal *Lobbying Act*, which requires lobbyists to register and adhere to rules of transparency regarding their activities.

### **Question 11**
**Explain how a "weaker" domestic currency affects a nation's Balance of Trade.**

**Answer:**
A "weaker" domestic currency means that the nation's currency buys less of another country's currency. This makes the nation's exports cheaper for foreign buyers to purchase and makes imports more expensive for domestic consumers. Consequently, a weaker currency should improve the balance of trade by encouraging exports and discouraging imports.

### **Question 12**
**Describe the "Cooperative" form of business ownership and its primary disadvantage regarding capital.**

**Answer:**
A Cooperative is an incorporated business that is owned and democratically controlled by the people who use its services (members). Earnings are distributed based on usage (patronage) rather than investment. A primary disadvantage is the difficulty in attracting equity investment; because returns are not tied to the level of investment, investors have little financial incentive to contribute large amounts of capital.

### **Question 13**
**Contrast "Intuition" and "Escalation of Commitment" as behavioral aspects of decision-making.**

**Answer:**
* **Intuition** is a behavioral aspect of decision-making where a manager makes a decision based on a "hunch" or feeling, usually derived from years of experience, rather than conscious logical analysis.
* **Escalation of Commitment** is a bias where a decision-maker remains committed to a bad decision and continues to invest resources in it, despite clear evidence that it was a mistake.

### **Question 14**
**List and briefly describe four types of variables used in "Market Segmentation."**

**Answer:**
1.  **Demographic Variables:** Characteristics of populations, such as age, income, gender, and ethnic background.
2.  **Geographic Variables:** Geographical units like countries, regions, cities, or neighborhoods.
3.  **Psychographic Variables:** Consumer characteristics such as lifestyles, opinions, interests, and attitudes.
4.  **Behavioural Variables:** Characteristics based on consumer knowledge, use, or response to a product, such as usage rate or loyalty status.

### **Question 15**
**Differentiate between a "Gantt Chart" and a "PERT Chart" in project scheduling.**

**Answer:**
* A **Gantt Chart** is a visual bar chart that diagrams the steps to be performed in a project and the time required for each step. It is useful for tracking progress against a timeline.
* A **PERT Chart** (Program Evaluation and Review Technique) is a diagram that shows the sequence of activities and identifies the "critical path"—the sequence of steps that will take the longest to complete. It is used to identify which tasks are critical to the project's total time.

### **Question 16**
**Define "Privatization" and "Deregulation" within the context of a mixed market economy.**

**Answer:**
* **Privatization** is the process of transferring government enterprises (such as Air Canada or Petro-Canada) to private ownership.
* **Deregulation** involves reducing the number of laws and regulations affecting business activity to encourage efficiency and competition. Both are features of a mixed market economy.

### **Question 17**
**What are "Core Competencies" and how do they relate to the strategy of "Outsourcing"?**

**Answer:**
Core competencies are the skills and resources with which a firm competes best and creates the most value. By focusing on these competencies, firms can identify non-essential business processes that do not add unique value. **Outsourcing** is the strategy of paying external suppliers to perform these non-core processes (like cafeteria operations in a museum), allowing the firm to focus its resources on its core competencies.

### **Question 18**
**Explain the "SMART" acronym used in goal setting.**

**Answer:**
Research shows that goals are most effective when they are **SMART**:
* **S**pecific: clearly defined.
* **M**easurable: quantifiable.
* **A**chievable: realistic to attain.
* **R**esults-oriented: focused on outcomes.
* **T**ime-framed: linked to a specific deadline.

### **Question 19**
**What is the purpose of "Auditing" in accounting, and which set of standards must public companies in Canada follow?**

**Answer:**
Auditing is an examination of a company's financial records to determine if they are accurate and comply with established accounting principles. Its purpose is to ensure the reliability of financial reporting. Public companies in Canada must follow the **International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)**.
